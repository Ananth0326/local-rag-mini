RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. It combines search with Large Language Models. First you retrieve relevant documents from a knowledge base. Then you feed those documents to an LLM to generate accurate answers. This prevents hallucination because the LLM answers based only on the retrieved text.

Vector embeddings are numerical representations of text. Similar texts have similar vectors. You can measure similarity using cosine distance.

Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors. A cosine of 1 means identical direction. A cosine of 0 means perpendicular. A cosine of -1 means opposite direction.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that improves LLM accuracy by providing relevant context from a knowledge base before generating an answer. The retrieval step finds documents similar to the user's question.

When you ask "what is RAG", the system converts your question to an embedding, then finds chunks with the most similar embeddings. The chunk containing "RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation" should have high similarity.
